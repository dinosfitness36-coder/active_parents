Implementation Plan

evaluation.htmlEvaluation

Objectives

  • Increase weekly moderate-to-vigorous physical activity among participating parents.
  • Reduce perceived barriers at individual, interpersonal, organizational, and community levels.
  • Establish sustainable community partnerships and family-oriented activity offers.

Phase 1 Months 0–3

  • Stakeholder mapping and MOUs (employers, community centres, councils).
  • Recruitment plan & safeguarding checks for facilitators.
  • Set up digital platform (sign-ups, reminders, resources).
  • Pilot scheduling & venue bookings.

Phase 2 Months 3–6

  • Run pilot: 6–8 weeks of family sessions + home workout packs.
  • Onboard employers to flexible-time challenges with incentives.
  • Weekly feedback loops; quick iterations on content & times.

Phase 3 Months 6–12

  • Scale to additional neighbourhoods; rotate venues for access.
  • Peer-led support groups; ambassador programme for parents.
  • Secure co-funding via sponsors/grants; integrate with local services.

Workstreams

  • Programme Delivery: Session design, facilitation, childcare support.
  • Community & Employers: Partnerships, scheduling, incentives.
  • Technology: Content library, reminders, basic progress tracking.
  • Marketing & Outreach: Posters, social media, GP/community referrals.
  • Safeguarding & Risk: Policies, training, incident logging.

Roles & Responsibilities (RACI)

  • Project Lead: Accountable (A) – governance, budget, risk.
  • Coordinator: Responsible (R) – operations, calendars, stakeholders.
  • Facilitators: Responsible (R) – session delivery & safety.
  • Data/Evaluation: Responsible (R) – KPIs, surveys, analysis; Consulted (C) with University partners.
  • Comms/Outreach: Responsible (R) – assets & campaigns; Informed (I) stakeholders.

Risks & Mitigations

  • Low attendance: Offer child-friendly times, incentives, partner referrals.
  • Venue availability: Pre-book blocks; maintain alternates.
  • Safeguarding incidents: Training, clear protocols, DBS checks.
  • Budget overrun: Contingency (10%), staged procurement.

Dependencies

  • Community centre schedules; employer policy alignment.
  • Equipment lead times; facilitator availability.
  • Data protection & consent materials (see Evaluation page).

Communications Plan

  • Parent onboarding pack (programme overview, schedule, consent).
  • Weekly reminders via SMS/email; monthly progress snapshot.
  • Stakeholder bulletin every 6–8 weeks with KPIs and stories.
